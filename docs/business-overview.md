# Business Overview

## Product Vision

We are building **Clarity** — an enterprise legal practice management platform for a modern legal firm. The system helps solicitors, consultants, legal assistants, finance teams, compliance teams, and administrators manage clients, matters, documents, time recording, billing, payments, compliance, reporting, and operational workflows in one secure platform.

## Why Clarity Exists

Legal firms operate in a complex environment where:

- Multiple stakeholders collaborate on client matters daily
- Strict compliance and regulatory requirements must be met
- Financial accuracy is critical for billing and payments
- Documents must be securely stored, versioned, and easily retrievable
- Time recording drives revenue and must be captured accurately
- Audit trails are legally required for accountability
- Management needs visibility into workloads, financials, and performance

Without a unified platform, firms rely on disconnected tools — spreadsheets for time tracking, email for document sharing, separate accounting systems, and manual compliance checks. This leads to lost revenue, compliance risk, poor client service, and inefficiency.

## What Business Problems Clarity Solves

| Problem | How Clarity Solves It |
|---------|----------------------|
| Lost billable time | Integrated time recording linked to matters |
| Missed compliance checks | Automated compliance workflows and audit logs |
| Scattered documents | Centralised document management per client/matter |
| Invoice disputes | Transparent billing with time entry detail |
| Poor visibility | Dashboards for management, finance, and compliance |
| Security risk | Role-based access control and encrypted data |
| Onboarding difficulty | Clear workflows guide new staff |
| Client dissatisfaction | Client portal with real-time matter visibility |

## Who Uses Clarity

Clarity serves the following user groups:

1. **Clients** — view their matters, upload documents, communicate with the legal team, view invoices, and make payments.
2. **Consultants / Solicitors** — manage matters, record time, upload documents, create notes, generate correspondence.
3. **Legal Assistants** — assist consultants, manage documentation, update matter information.
4. **Team Leaders** — monitor teams, review workloads, approve activities.
5. **Finance Team** — manage invoices, process payments, produce financial reports.
6. **Compliance Officers** — review audit logs, perform compliance checks, investigate flagged activity.
7. **System Administrators** — manage users, permissions, and platform configuration.
8. **Support Users** — assist internal users with platform issues and troubleshooting.

## Core Business Modules

Clarity is organised into the following business modules:

1. **Client Management** — creating and managing client records, contacts, and relationships.
2. **Matter Management** — the heart of the platform; managing legal cases from opening to closure.
3. **Document Management** — uploading, storing, versioning, and retrieving documents.
4. **Time Recording** — capturing billable and non-billable time against matters.
5. **Billing** — generating invoices from time entries and disbursements.
6. **Payments** — recording and reconciling payments received.
7. **Tasks and Workflows** — assigning, tracking, and completing work items.
8. **Compliance** — monitoring regulatory adherence and flagging issues.
9. **Audit Logs** — recording every significant action for accountability.
10. **Reporting** — dashboards and exports for management decision-making.
11. **Administration** — user management, roles, permissions, and system configuration.
12. **Notifications** — alerts and reminders for deadlines, tasks, and events.

## How Revenue is Generated

The legal firm generates revenue by:

- Charging clients for billable hours worked on matters
- Charging fixed fees for certain matter types
- Passing through disbursements (third-party costs)
- Potentially charging for additional services

Clarity ensures every billable minute is captured and converted into revenue through accurate invoicing.

## Business Criticality

This platform is **business-critical**. If Clarity is unavailable:

- Solicitors cannot record time (lost revenue)
- Finance cannot generate invoices (cash flow impact)
- Compliance checks are missed (regulatory risk)
- Clients cannot access their matter information (service failure)
- Management loses visibility into operations

The platform must be reliable, performant, and available during business hours with minimal downtime.
