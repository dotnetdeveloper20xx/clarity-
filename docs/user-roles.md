# User Roles

This document describes each user role in the Clarity platform, including their daily activities, information needs, permitted actions, and restrictions.

---

## 1. Client

### What They Do Daily
- Check the status of their legal matters
- Upload documents requested by their legal team
- Communicate with their assigned solicitor or legal assistant
- Review and pay invoices
- View correspondence and notes shared with them

### Information They Need
- Current status of each matter
- Outstanding invoices and payment history
- Documents related to their matters
- Messages from the legal team
- Upcoming deadlines or required actions

### Actions They Can Perform
- View their own matters and matter history
- Upload documents to their matters
- Send and receive messages
- View invoices
- Make payments
- Update their own contact information

### What They Cannot Do
- View other clients' information
- Modify matter details
- Access internal notes or time entries
- Delete documents
- Access billing configuration or financial reports
- Access any administrative functions

---

## 2. Consultant / Solicitor

### What They Do Daily
- Work on client matters (legal research, drafting, advising)
- Record time spent on each matter
- Upload and review documents
- Create notes and correspondence
- Communicate with clients
- Manage their task list
- Review upcoming deadlines

### Information They Need
- List of assigned matters and their status
- Client details for active matters
- Documents associated with matters
- Time entries recorded
- Outstanding tasks and deadlines
- Team communications

### Actions They Can Perform
- View and update assigned matters
- Record time entries (billable and non-billable)
- Upload and download documents
- Create notes and correspondence
- Assign tasks to legal assistants
- Update matter status
- Communicate with clients

### What They Cannot Do
- Create or delete clients (only administrators)
- Approve invoices or process payments
- Access finance reports
- Modify billing rates
- Access compliance review tools
- Manage users or permissions
- View matters not assigned to them (unless team leader)

---

## 3. Legal Assistant

### What They Do Daily
- Support consultants with administrative tasks
- Manage and organise documents
- Update matter information
- Chase outstanding items
- Coordinate with clients for document collection
- Prepare bundles and correspondence

### Information They Need
- Matters they are assisting with
- Outstanding tasks assigned to them
- Documents awaiting processing
- Client contact details
- Consultant instructions and notes

### Actions They Can Perform
- View assigned matters
- Upload and organise documents
- Update matter details (non-financial)
- Complete assigned tasks
- Create notes
- Communicate with clients on behalf of consultants

### What They Cannot Do
- Record billable time (only non-billable)
- Approve or modify invoices
- Change matter status to closed
- Access financial dashboards
- Manage users or permissions
- Access compliance tools

---

## 4. Team Leader

### What They Do Daily
- Monitor their team's workload and capacity
- Review matter progress across the team
- Approve time entries and activities
- Identify bottlenecks and redistribute work
- Attend management meetings with data from the platform

### Information They Need
- Team member workloads
- Matter status across their team
- Time recording summaries
- Overdue tasks and deadlines
- Performance metrics

### Actions They Can Perform
- View all matters assigned to their team
- Approve or reject time entries
- Reassign matters and tasks
- View team dashboards and reports
- Escalate compliance concerns
- All actions a Consultant can perform

### What They Cannot Do
- Access other teams' data (unless granted)
- Approve invoices or process payments
- Manage platform-wide settings
- Delete clients or matters
- Access system administration

---

## 5. Finance User

### What They Do Daily
- Generate invoices from approved time entries
- Process payments received from clients
- Reconcile accounts
- Chase overdue invoices
- Produce financial reports
- Manage billing rates and disbursements

### Information They Need
- Approved time entries ready for billing
- Outstanding invoices and aging reports
- Payment records
- Client billing history
- Revenue and cash flow summaries

### Actions They Can Perform
- Generate and send invoices
- Record payments received
- Mark invoices as paid, partially paid, or written off
- Manage billing rates
- Produce financial reports and exports
- View matter financial summaries

### What They Cannot Do
- Modify matter details (non-financial)
- Record time entries
- Access document content
- Manage users or permissions
- Perform compliance reviews
- Close or open matters

---

## 6. Compliance Officer

### What They Do Daily
- Review audit logs for suspicious or unusual activity
- Perform compliance checks on matters (e.g., anti-money laundering)
- Investigate flagged activities
- Produce compliance reports
- Ensure regulatory requirements are met

### Information They Need
- Audit trail for all significant actions
- Flagged matters or activities
- Compliance check status per matter
- User activity reports
- Regulatory requirement checklists

### Actions They Can Perform
- View all audit logs
- Flag matters for investigation
- Complete compliance checks
- Produce compliance reports
- View any matter's compliance history
- Escalate issues to management

### What They Cannot Do
- Modify matter details
- Record time or generate invoices
- Delete any records
- Manage users or permissions
- Access client payment information
- Upload or modify documents

---

## 7. System Administrator

### What They Do Daily
- Manage user accounts (create, disable, update roles)
- Configure platform settings
- Manage permissions and access control
- Monitor system health
- Support other users with access issues
- Apply platform updates and configuration changes

### Information They Need
- User account list and statuses
- Role and permission assignments
- System health and performance metrics
- Configuration settings
- Error logs and diagnostics

### Actions They Can Perform
- Create, update, and disable user accounts
- Assign and revoke roles
- Configure system settings
- View system health dashboards
- Manage reference data (matter types, fee earner grades, etc.)
- Reset passwords and unlock accounts

### What They Cannot Do
- Access client matter content (unless also assigned another role)
- Modify financial records
- Approve invoices or process payments
- Delete audit logs
- Bypass compliance requirements

---

## 8. Support User

### What They Do Daily
- Assist internal users with platform issues
- Investigate reported problems
- Check system logs for errors
- Escalate issues to administrators or developers
- Document known issues and workarounds

### Information They Need
- User-reported issues
- System logs and error traces
- Platform health metrics
- Known issues documentation
- User activity context (for troubleshooting)

### Actions They Can Perform
- View system logs and diagnostics
- View user activity (for troubleshooting)
- Create support tickets
- Access knowledge base and documentation
- Impersonate users for investigation (with audit trail)

### What They Cannot Do
- Modify user accounts or permissions
- Access financial data
- Modify matters or documents
- Process payments
- Delete any records
